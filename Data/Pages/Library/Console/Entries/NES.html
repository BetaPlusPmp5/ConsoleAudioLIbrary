Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)

The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), originally launched as the Famicom in Japan in 1983, was released in North America on October 18, 1985. It marked Nintendo's entry into the global home console market and played a critical role in reviving the video game industry after the 1983 crash.

The system was manufactured and published by Nintendo Co., Ltd. and helped standardize the modern console model with licensing, third-party development, and region locks. It was distributed in Europe and Australia under various regional partnerships, including Mattel and Bergsala.

Bundled with Super Mario Bros., the NES became a household name due to its iconic characters, tight gameplay, and accessible design. It set the foundation for many of the gaming franchises we still know today:

Final Fantasy

Super Mario Bros. 3

Metroid

The NES sold over 61.91 million units worldwide, making it one of the most successful consoles of its time. It helped Nintendo establish long-term dominance in the gaming market and fostered a generation of game designers and players.

🧠 Hardware & Capabilities

  • CPU: Ricoh 2A03 (NTSC) / 2A07 (PAL), based on 6502
  • Clock Speed: 1.79 MHz (NTSC), 1.66 MHz (PAL)
  • Resolution: 256×240 (NTSC)
  • Color Palette: 48 colors, 5 grays (up to 25 on screen)
  • Sprites: 64 sprites total, 8 per scanline
  • Cartridge Sizes: Ranged from 192 KB to 4 MB (with mappers)

🌍 Regional Variants

The NES and Famicom had notable differences across regions:

  • Japan: Launched as the Famicom in red and white casing, top-loading with hardwired controllers
  • North America: Featured a front-loading "VCR" design with rectangular controllers
  • Europe: Used the PAL version with different timing, and featured both NES and Famicom-style variants

📽️ Long-Term Legacy

The NES is credited with reviving the home console market in North America and shaping game design principles that persist today. Many gaming icons like Mario, Samus, and Link were born on this system.

It was re-released in 2016 as the Nintendo Classic Mini: NES, featuring 30 pre-installed games and selling out globally within days.

To this day, IGN ranks the NES as one of the most influential consoles of all time for its role in shaping video game history.
