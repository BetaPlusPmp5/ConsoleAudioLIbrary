Welcome to The Console audio Library

Welcome to the Console Audio Library** – the definitive destination for anyone seeking accurate, detailed, and engaging information on the audio systems of retro video game consoles. Whether you're a developer, audio engineer, music producer, or enthusiast, our platform serves as your trusted, user-friendly resource for everything related to classic game audio.

At the Console Audio Library, we focus on the sounds and music from old video game consoles — like the ones from the 8-bit and 16-bit eras. These consoles had limited technology, so game developers had to get creative to make music and sound effects. We explore how those classic sounds were made, what tools were used, and why they still matter today.

What can you expect on this library

Discover in-depth information about classic game consoles, from their hardware specs to their unique sound capabilities.

Learn how sound chips like the SN76489 or SPC700 worked, and how composers of the time pushed the limits of early audio technology

Access a curated list of digital tools, VST plugins, and trackers that replicate vintage sound chips for modern use.

Dive into scanned manuals, old developer documentation, technical breakdowns, and helpful diagrams.

Beginner-friendly tutorials that explain key concepts like FM synthesis, pulse waves, and sample looping.
